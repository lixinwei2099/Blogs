A Junior Developer's Frustration ‚Äã

I'm a busy person these days. I have a lot of work to do, including fixing numerous bugs. My goal is to launch my blog by the end of this month, but it's already May 28th and I'm worried that I won't be able to finish everything on time. I'm feeling exhausted and stressed out, but my colleague tells me to take it easy and not rush everything. She says that if I can't finish my work today, I can always do it tomorrow. I'm trying to learn to fix bugs in a relaxed way, without pushing myself too hard.

However, I must admit that when my leader assigns me to fix a bug, I feel a bit fearful. I worry that I won't be able to do it well. I wonder why he doesn't fix the bugs himself - maybe it's because it's difficult to find the issues in the code, especially since there are thousands of lines of code written by other developers. Sometimes he gives me tasks he doesn't want to do himself. If I tell him that I can't fix a bug, he might insult me and make me feel terrible. But I don't care what he says - I'm just here to make a living.

From now on, I've decided to prioritize completing my admin project and finishing my computer hardware course. Over the next few days, I plan to enjoy the end of this month without pushing myself too hard. I'll take a more relaxed approach and not be too strict with myself. I hope to take things one at a time and work slowly and steadily. I believe that one day I will be able to do well and become an excellent programmer.

Endless and Exhausting Meetings ‚Äã

I find myself exhausted due to the endless meetings I have to attend at work. This month, I am required to join meetings every week, even after finishing work at 8 pm. Attending these meetings leaves me feeling tired and breathless. It's frustrating because I need some rest after work, but instead, I have to attend these long and often unproductive meetings.

In the mornings, I sit down at my workstation, and within a few minutes, the leader will ask me to join a meeting. As a junior front-end developer, I feel confused about why he wants me to participate in the database establishment. I don't think I have the required skills, and I'm not sure if this is a standard work process.

The truth is, we only have five developers in our department, and everyone is so busy fixing bugs assigned by the leader. I believe the leader asked me to join because there is no one else available. He said it would help me understand the database, but I feel that I only need to know about the table relationships, and I don't need to refactor the entire database.

During these meetings, we discuss endlessly from 10 am to 8 pm, with very little progress made. The efficiency is low, and when I make a mistake, the leader insults me. I hope we can finish this meeting quickly, but unfortunately, we haven't accomplished much yet. We still need to discuss the database next week.
