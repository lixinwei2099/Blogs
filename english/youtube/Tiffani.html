SMALL TALK ‚Äã

  • Small talk is basically meant to be light and fun.

    It is a way for 2 (or more)people to talk to each other,without really getting to know the other.

  • In this type of conversation,no personal questions are usually asked. Usually the only emotions involved are happy or neutral. Does not lead to the individuals truly getting to know each other.

DEEP CONVERSATION ‚Äã

  • Deep conversation is meant to be deep and thought provoking.

    It is a way for 2(or more) people to talk to each other and truly understand how the other thinks

  • Enables the individuals to truly get to know each other on a deeper level. It usually requires deep thought and a certain opinion to be expressed. Allows a person to express their true feelings about a certain topic.

TIP

üòÄ Conversation is back and forth.

Here are three things you should remember :

DON'T DOMINATE ‚Äã

The first thing to remember is to never dominate the conversation. It is important to make sure there is a balance between you and the person you are speaking to.

  1. Dominating shows that you don't care about the other person's opinion.
  2. People don't want to have conversations with people who dominate the conversation.
  3. It also shows that your listening skills are not good
LISTEN ATTENTIVELY ‚Äã

The second thing to remember is that listening skills are an important part of effective communication. In other words, listening attentively will actually help you to master English conversation.

  1. Listening attentively will help you to understand people better.
  2. As you listen,you actually improve your English ability, because you are taking in new information
  3. If you listen well, the other person will also want to listen to you.
DON'T INTERRUPT ‚Äã

The third thing to remember is to never interrupt(ÊâìÊñ≠). This is because interrupting someone,or suddenly stopping them,is rude and can stop a conversation.

  1. People don't want to talk to someone who constantly interrupts them.
  2. Interrupting someone shows that you are not listening to them.
  3. It also hinders the flow of conversation, because it shows that you think your words are more important.
TYPE EXPERIENCE ‚Äã
  1. Say the name of the person you are speaking with. Example "Well Brian,it was so great speaking with you..."
  2. Briefly mention the experience they told you about again. Example "I really enjoyed hearing about your trip to Paris..."
  3. Relate it to yourself and something about the future very quickly. Example"Hopefully I will be able to visit there in the future as well..."
example ‚Äã
  • Well Tiff,it has been amazing speaking with you. I can imagine how much fun you got with your sister. may be when my sister come back we will try rapping together.
  • Well Alex,it was so great speaking with you. I really enjoyed hearing about your games. Hopefully I will be able to play some funny video games with you.
TYPE FEELINGS ‚Äã
  1. Say the name of the person you are speaking with. Example "Wow Mary,I truly enjoyed speaking with you..."
  2. Briefly mention the feelings they told you about again. Example"It was very interesting listening to how you felt about..."
  3. Mention the importance of that feeling and lead into another future conversation. Example"I also believe it is very important. Hopefully we can talk more about it soon..."
